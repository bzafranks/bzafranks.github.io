Behavior Documentation

Capture what happened with clarity and care. Describe the facts, and the assistant drafts neutral, professional documentation you can refine — so your records stay objective and your relationships stay intact.

Generate objective, factual incident reports, behavior reflections, restorative conversation guides, positive recognition notes, and behavior intervention plans. Every draft records observable behavior without labels or judgment, names the antecedent and response, and offers a partnership-oriented family summary — all aligned to PBIS and restorative practices.

Drafts are starting points. Review for accuracy and follow your school's documentation, privacy, and reporting policies before sharing.
