Overview

Not an accreditation checklist — a complete quality-assurance system

Accreditation done well is continuous, evidence-driven, and improvement-focused. This system unifies standards, readiness, evidence, continuous improvement, and quality analytics so accreditation becomes a byproduct of how the school operates every day.

Why Schools Struggle

From last-minute scramble to everyday quality

⏳ Compliance Scramble

Evidence is assembled in a panic every cycle instead of collected continuously.

🗂️ Scattered Evidence

Artifacts live in inboxes and drives with no alignment to standards.

🔌 Disconnected from Improvement

Accreditation runs parallel to — not as part of — school improvement.

Research Foundation

Grounded in quality-assurance & continuous-improvement research

The framework integrates Continuous Improvement theory, Quality Assurance principles, Organizational Effectiveness, Systems Thinking, and accreditation best practice (Cognia, CIS, WASC, Middle States, ISO) — with a through-line to Dr. Franks' professional experience leading accreditation initiatives, quality reviews, and institutional effectiveness.
