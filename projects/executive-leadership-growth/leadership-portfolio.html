Leadership Philosophy

🧭 A statement of leadership belief

“Leadership is the disciplined act of growing people. I lead so that teachers can teach with confidence, students can learn without limits, and the school can improve without burning out. I anchor every decision in a shared vision, distribute authority to those closest to the work, and keep equity, evidence, and trust at the center of how we make choices together.”

Leadership Reflections

💭 On distributing leadership

Sharing authority felt risky at first; the turning point was watching teacher-led teams solve problems I had owned alone. Trust, once given with structure, multiplied capacity across the building.

💭 On protecting instructional time

The hardest leadership work was saying no to good ideas to protect time for the highest-leverage few. Streamlining operations bought back four hours a week for walkthroughs and feedback.
