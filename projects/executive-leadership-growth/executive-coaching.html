🎓 Reflective Leadership Coaching

Executive Coaching

A structured, reflective coaching cycle that turns feedback and goals into focused leadership growth — supported by a thinking partner, a clear conversation structure, and a private reflection journal. All examples are illustrative.

The executive coaching cycle

A repeating cycle that pairs reflection with action and keeps a leader's growth visible and accountable.

  • Step 1
    Reflect — review 360° feedback, the self-assessment, and recent leadership moments to surface what matters most.
  • Step 2
    Set goals — name one or two high-leverage growth goals tied to a leadership domain.
  • Step 3 · current
    Coaching conversation — meet with a coach as a thinking partner using the GROW structure below.
  • Step 4
    Action — apply a small set of high-leverage practices between sessions.
  • Step 5
    Journal — capture reflections after key leadership moments to track learning.
  • Step 6
    Progress review — gather evidence, review impact, and set the next cycle.

Coaching-conversation structure (GROW)

A simple, durable structure for a focused coaching conversation between a leader and a coach.

G · GoalWhat do you most want from this conversation? What does success look like for this cycle?
R · RealityWhat is happening now? What does your feedback and evidence tell you? What have you tried?
O · OptionsWhat could you do? What would a bold move look like? What is one small first step?
W · WillWhat will you commit to, by when? How will you know it worked? What support do you need?

🤝 Accountability supports

  • Bi-weekly conversations with a coaching thinking partner.
  • A small set of indicators the leader can monitor between sessions.
  • Reflection journaling after key leadership moments (below).
  • Portfolio evidence linking actions to leadership impact.
  • Mid-point check-ins to adjust the plan while there is still time.
