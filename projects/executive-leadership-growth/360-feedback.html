📋 Generated feedback summary — click each section to expand

👤 Synthesized summary Themes drawn from aggregated, anonymized responses. Illustrative sample data.

▸🌟 Leadership strengthsSignature
  • People Leadership (92%) — every source names trust, recognition, and care for staff as a defining strength.
  • Ethical Leadership (90%) — decisions are seen as values-based, transparent, and equity-centered.
  • Visionary Leadership (87%) — the school community can articulate a shared, compelling direction.
▸🎯 Growth opportunitiesFocus
  • Strategic Leadership (75%) — sources want clearer prioritization and resource alignment to a few high-leverage goals.
  • Operational Leadership (78%) — parents and self-ratings point to streamlining systems and communication routines.
  • Self-perception gap — self-ratings run below others' ratings on Strategic and Instructional; an opportunity to own strengths.
▸📈 Leadership trendsImproving
  • Favorable rating rose from 82% last cycle to 88% this cycle (▲6).
  • Teacher and leadership-team confidence is consistently the highest of all sources.
  • Strategic Leadership, while lowest, gained ground after a 90-day prioritization focus.
▸🧭 Professional recommendationsAction
  • Open a coaching cycle on Strategic Leadership; narrow to two high-leverage priorities.
  • Co-design an operations routine with the leadership team to protect instructional time.
  • Share quarterly progress with families to close the parent-communication gap.
▸🌱 Personal development prioritiesNext cycle
  • Reflect on the strength-recognition gap between self and others through journaling.
  • Translate feedback into a measurable 90-day growth plan with evidence indicators.
  • Sustain People & Ethical Leadership behaviors while building Strategic capacity.
