Universal Design for Learning

Every lesson is planned across all three UDL principles so it works for the whole class without retrofitting later.

💛 Multiple Means of Engagement

  • Offer choice of "small moment" topic from students' known interests (e.g., basketball, LEGO, ocean animals, graphic novels) so every student has an authentic story to tell.
  • Open with a relatable mentor text to build interest before independent writing begins.
  • Provide a visual progress tracker so students can see each planning step move them closer to a finished draft.
  • Build in low-stakes partner rehearsal before writing, reducing anxiety around getting started.

🟢 Multiple Means of Representation

  • Present the mentor text in print, audio read-aloud, and think-aloud video-annotated formats.
  • Model the graphic organizer with a completed visual example, not just a blank template.
  • Pre-teach key vocabulary (sensory detail, point of view, sequence) with icons and one-sentence, student-friendly definitions.
  • Provide sentence starters and a sensory-detail word bank for students who need support generating language.

🟠 Multiple Means of Action & Expression

  • Offer response-format choice: type, handwrite, or dictate the opening paragraph using speech-to-text.
  • Provide a chunked, one-box-at-a-time version of the graphic organizer for students with executive-functioning needs.
  • Allow extended time and a reduced-distraction workspace during independent drafting.
  • Give students a self-checklist in the Success Criteria language so they can monitor their own progress before submitting.
