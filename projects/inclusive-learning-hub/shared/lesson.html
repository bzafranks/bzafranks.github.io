📚 Module 3 · Grade 7 ELA

Writing a Personal Narrative

Students plan, draft, and revise a personal narrative with a clear sequence of events, sensory detail, and a reflective conclusion.

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Sequence the events of a true story in a clear beginning, middle, and end.
  • Use sensory details (sight, sound, smell, touch, taste) to help the reader picture the moment.
  • Include dialogue and interior thought to show — not just tell — how a moment felt.
  • Write a reflective conclusion that explains why the moment mattered.

🧠 Key Vocabulary

  • Narrative — a story that has a beginning, middle, and end.
  • Sequence — the order that events happen in, from first to last.
  • Sensory detail — a description that uses one of your five senses so the reader can picture, hear, or feel the moment.
  • Dialogue — the exact words characters say, shown in quotation marks.
  • Reflection — thinking back on an experience and explaining what it meant or what you learned.
  • Transition — a word or phrase (like "later that day" or "finally") that helps the reader move smoothly from one event to the next.

👁️ Visual Examples

Weak Beginning

"I played basketball. It was fun."

Strong Beginning (Sensory Detail)

"My sneakers squeaked against the gym floor as the whistle blew and my heart pounded."

Reflective Ending

"That missed shot taught me that one moment doesn't decide who I am as a teammate."

🧑‍🏫 Teacher Instructions

Pacing: One 45-minute class period for video + transcript + vocabulary, one period for the sequencing activity + drafting, one period for revision using the response chooser below.

Common misconception: Students often summarize ("I went to the game and we won") instead of narrating a single moment in detail. Prompt students to "slow down" the most exciting 60 seconds of their story.

UDL note: The sequencing activity, read-aloud option, and response chooser below give students multiple means of engagement and expression without changing the grade-level standard (W.7.3).

Accommodation guidance: For students with Chunked Assignments, split drafting into three separate mini-tasks (beginning, middle, end) rather than one long draft. For students with Graphic Organizers, require the planning worksheet before drafting begins.

🧭 Student Directions

  1. Watch the lesson video (or use Read-Aloud to listen to the transcript).
  2. Review the Key Vocabulary terms — you'll use them in your own writing.
  3. Complete the "Put the Events in Order" activity to practice sequencing.
  4. Download and fill out the planning worksheet for your own true story.
  5. Choose how you'd like to respond below, then submit your work.

🛟 Support Options

  • Need more time? Ask your teacher about Extended Time for this assignment.
  • Want the lesson read to you? Use the Read-Aloud button above at any point.
  • Need help planning? Ask for a Graphic Organizer before you start drafting.
  • Feeling overwhelmed by the whole draft? Ask about Chunked Assignments — beginning, middle, and end as three smaller steps.
