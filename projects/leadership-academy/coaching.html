🧑‍🏫 Coaching System

Coaching & Mentoring

Great leaders are grown, not born. This coaching system pairs executive coaching, peer mentoring, and structured reflection with a saved coaching plan and a private reflection journal — so growth is intentional, tracked, and sustained over time.

Coaching & Mentoring Models

Tap a model to explore how it works and when to use it.

▸🤝Executive coaching1:1 · confidential

A confidential, goal-oriented partnership with an experienced coach. The leader sets the agenda; the coach uses powerful questions, evidence, and accountability to accelerate growth. Typical cadence: bi-weekly 45–60 minute sessions over a school year.

▸👥Peer mentoringcohort · reciprocal

Leaders at similar stages learn alongside one another, trading practice, problems of practice, and feedback. Reciprocity builds trust and reduces isolation — one of the strongest predictors of leader retention.

▸💬Coaching conversationsin-the-moment

Short, structured conversations a leader can use with their own team: name the situation, ask before telling, co-create next steps, and close with a clear commitment. Builds a coaching culture across the organization.

▸🌱GROW modelGoal · Reality · Options · Will

Goal — what do you want to achieve? Reality — what is happening now? Options — what could you do? Will — what will you do, by when? A simple, durable structure for any coaching conversation.

The Coaching Cycle

  • Step 1
    Establish the relationship — build trust, clarify confidentiality, and agree on how you will work together.
  • Step 2
    Set goals — define meaningful, measurable leadership goals tied to real outcomes.
  • Step 3
    Coaching conversations — regular sessions using GROW, evidence, and reflection.
  • Step 4
    Take action — leadership action plans turn insight into on-the-job practice.
  • Step 5
    Progress reviews — revisit goals, celebrate growth, and re-focus the plan.

Reflection Journal

Reflection turns experience into learning. Capture a weekly entry — entries stay private in this browser.

Research-informed

Studies of school leadership consistently find that ongoing, individualized coaching and mentoring — rather than one-off workshops — produces the largest, most durable gains in leadership practice and reduces principal turnover. Reflection and goal-setting amplify the effect by helping leaders convert experience into deliberate practice.
