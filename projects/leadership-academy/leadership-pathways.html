Leadership Pathways

Every leader is on a journey. These seven pathways map the road from aspiration to transformation — each with a learning roadmap, required competencies, recommended courses, milestones, assessment checkpoints, and certification outcomes. Select a pathway to expand its plan.

The Leadership Journey

Aspire

Build identity, foundational knowledge, and readiness for the first leadership role.

Lead

Stabilize, set direction, and lead instruction, people, and operations with confidence.

Develop

Deepen expertise, develop others, and drive sustained school and system improvement.

Transform

Shape vision, policy, and culture at scale — building leaders and lasting institutions.

Choose Your Pathway

🌱

Aspiring School Leaders

Teacher leaders and assistant principals preparing for their first school leadership role.

Learning roadmap

  1. Months 1–2 · Aspire
    Clarify leadership purpose, values, and your readiness profile.
  2. Months 3–5 · Aspire
    Foundations of leadership, school systems, and adult learning.
  3. Months 6–8 · Lead
    Lead a school-improvement project under mentor supervision.

Required competencies

Visionary Leadership · Instructional Leadership · People Leadership · Ethical Leadership.

Recommended courses

Foundations of Educational Leadership · School Culture & Climate · Data-Informed Leadership.

Leadership milestones

Complete a leadership readiness self-assessment; lead a grade-level or department initiative; shadow a sitting principal.

Assessment checkpoints

Entry diagnostic · mid-pathway portfolio review · capstone leadership project defense.

Certification outcomes

Aspiring Leader Credential — readiness for assistant principal / first-role candidacy.

🧭

New Principals

First- and second-year principals navigating entry, stabilization, and early wins.

Learning roadmap

  1. Days 1–90 · Lead
    Entry plan, listening tour, and first-90-days priorities.
  2. Term 1 · Lead
    Establish instructional routines, safety, and staff trust.
  3. Year 1 · Lead
    Set a shared vision and a measurable improvement goal.

Required competencies

Instructional Leadership · People Leadership · Financial & Operational Leadership · Ethical Leadership.

Recommended courses

Foundations of Educational Leadership · Instructional Leadership Excellence · Teacher Retention & Workforce Leadership.

Leadership milestones

Publish an entry plan; complete a full observation cycle; stabilize staffing and the master schedule.

Assessment checkpoints

Entry-plan review · 90-day reflection · end-of-year leadership evaluation.

Certification outcomes

New Principal Certificate — verified first-year leadership competence.

🏫

Experienced Principals

Veteran school leaders deepening instructional impact and developing other leaders.

Learning roadmap

  1. Phase 1 · Develop
    Advanced instructional leadership and coaching at scale.
  2. Phase 2 · Develop
    Build a leadership pipeline and distribute leadership.
  3. Phase 3 · Transform
    Lead whole-school improvement and culture change.

Required competencies

Instructional Leadership · Strategic Leadership · People Leadership · Innovation Leadership.

Recommended courses

Instructional Leadership Excellence · Leading School Improvement · Leading Change.

Leadership milestones

Mentor an aspiring leader; lead a sustained improvement cycle; present at a system convening.

Assessment checkpoints

Leadership impact review · 360° feedback cycle · improvement-results audit.

Certification outcomes

Master Principal Credential — advanced instructional leadership recognition.

🗺️

District Leaders

Central-office leaders and superintendents aligning systems across many schools.

Learning roadmap

  1. Phase 1 · Develop
    System diagnosis, theory of action, and strategic alignment.
  2. Phase 2 · Transform
    Coherent improvement, resource equity, and accountability.
  3. Phase 3 · Transform
    Build leadership capacity across the system.

Required competencies

Strategic Leadership · Financial & Operational Leadership · People Leadership · Community Leadership.

Recommended courses

Strategic Planning · Leading School Improvement · Data-Informed Leadership.

Leadership milestones

Author a district strategic plan; lead a multi-school improvement network; pass an equity audit.

Assessment checkpoints

Strategic-plan review · mid-cycle systems audit · board-facing impact report.

Certification outcomes

System Leadership Credential — readiness for cabinet / superintendency.

👔

    Executive presence, governance, and stakeholder leadership.
  2. Phase 2 · Transform
    Organizational strategy, finance, and sustainable growth.
  3. Phase 3 · Transform
    Succession, board development, and legacy.

Required competencies

Visionary Leadership · Strategic Leadership · Financial & Operational Leadership · Innovation Leadership.

Recommended courses

Strategic Planning · Leading Change · Educational Innovation.

Leadership milestones

Lead a board through strategy; deliver a multi-year financial sustainability plan; develop a successor.

Assessment checkpoints

Executive 360° review · governance audit · organizational health survey.

Certification outcomes

Executive Leadership Credential — advanced organizational leadership recognition.

🏛️

Required competencies

Recommended courses

🌍

International School Leaders

Leaders of international and multicultural schools navigating cross-cultural contexts.

Learning roadmap

  1. Phase 1 · Lead
    Cross-cultural leadership and global accreditation standards.
  2. Phase 2 · Develop
    Lead multinational staff, families, and boards.
  3. Phase 3 · Transform
    Build globally minded, sustainable school communities.

Required competencies

Community Leadership · People Leadership · Instructional Leadership · Ethical Leadership.

Recommended courses

School Culture & Climate · Instructional Leadership Excellence · Teacher Retention & Workforce Leadership.

Leadership milestones

Lead an accreditation cycle; build a cross-cultural staff retention plan; host a global community forum.

Assessment checkpoints

Accreditation self-study · cultural-climate survey · governance & outcomes review.

Certification outcomes

International School Leadership Credential — cross-cultural leadership recognition.

Research note

Effective leadership development is staged, experiential, and grounded in adult-learning principles: adults learn best when content is relevant to a real role, builds on prior experience, and is applied immediately on the job. These pathways therefore blend coursework with authentic projects, mentoring, and reflective practice, and revisit core competencies at increasing depth as leaders move from Aspire to Transform.
