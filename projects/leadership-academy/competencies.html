🎯 Eight Domains of Leadership

Leadership Competency Framework

The competencies that define effective educational leaders, organized into eight interconnected domains. Select any competency to reveal its definition, the leadership behaviors it requires, evidence of mastery, development activities, suggested resources, and how it grows over time.

The Eight Competencies

Click a card to expand its detail.

🔭 Visionary Leadership

Direction-setting · Domain 1 of 8

Definition. Creating, communicating, and sustaining a shared, future-focused vision that inspires collective action.

Leadership behaviors. Articulates a compelling vision; aligns goals to it; tells a clear story of where the school is headed and why.

Evidence of mastery. Staff and families can describe the vision; decisions and budgets visibly align to it.

Development activities. Draft a one-page vision; facilitate a visioning workshop; audit decisions for alignment.

Suggested resources. Strategic Planning · vision-casting masterclass · stakeholder communication toolkit.

Growth progression. Emerging states a vision → Transforming embeds it across the system.

📚 Instructional Leadership

Teaching & learning · Domain 2 of 8

Definition. Leading the improvement of teaching and learning so every student achieves at high levels.

Leadership behaviors. Conducts walkthroughs; gives actionable feedback; protects instructional time; leads on curriculum and assessment.

Evidence of mastery. Rising student outcomes; consistent high-quality instruction; teachers cite useful feedback.

Development activities. Calibrated observation cycles; lead a PLC; co-plan a unit with a teacher.

Suggested resources. Instructional Leadership Excellence · observation rubric · coaching playbook.

Growth progression. Emerging observes → Transforming builds a self-improving instructional system.

♟️ Strategic Leadership

Planning & priorities · Domain 3 of 8

Definition. Translating vision into a coherent, prioritized strategy with clear goals, resources, and accountability.

Leadership behaviors. Sets a theory of action; sequences priorities; monitors progress and adapts.

Evidence of mastery. A living strategic plan; goals met on schedule; tradeoffs made deliberately.

Development activities. Build a strategy map; run a quarterly review; conduct a root-cause analysis.

Suggested resources. Strategic Planning · Leading School Improvement · OKR template.

Growth progression. Emerging writes a plan → Transforming drives coherent system strategy.

🤝 People Leadership

Talent & teams · Domain 4 of 8

Definition. Recruiting, developing, retaining, and empowering people to do their best work.

Leadership behaviors. Hires well; coaches; delegates; builds trust; distributes leadership.

Evidence of mastery. High retention of effective staff; strong engagement; a visible leadership pipeline.

Development activities. Run a stay interview; build a development plan; mentor an aspiring leader.

Suggested resources. Teacher Retention & Workforce Leadership · coaching course · feedback frameworks.

Growth progression. Emerging manages tasks → Transforming grows other leaders.

🏗️ Financial & Operational Leadership

Resources & systems · Domain 5 of 8

Definition. Stewarding budgets, facilities, schedules, and systems so resources serve the mission.

Leadership behaviors. Aligns budget to priorities; builds efficient systems; manages risk and compliance.

Evidence of mastery. Balanced, equity-driven budgets; smooth operations; resources reach classrooms.

Development activities. Build a priority-based budget; audit a key process; design the master schedule.

Suggested resources. Data-Informed Leadership · budgeting toolkit · operations checklist.

Growth progression. Emerging manages a budget → Transforming ensures system-wide resource equity.

🌐 Community Leadership

Family & partnerships · Domain 6 of 8

Definition. Building genuine partnerships with families, communities, and stakeholders to support students.

Leadership behaviors. Listens to families; engages diverse voices; communicates transparently; builds coalitions.

Evidence of mastery. Strong family engagement; trusted community relationships; partners invested in the school.

Development activities. Host a community forum; map stakeholders; co-create a family-engagement plan.

Suggested resources. School Culture & Climate · engagement survey · communications planner.

Growth progression. Emerging informs families → Transforming co-leads with the community.

⚖️ Ethical Leadership

Integrity & equity · Domain 7 of 8

Definition. Leading with integrity, equity, and care, modeling values and holding to them under pressure.

Leadership behaviors. Acts on values; ensures equity; makes principled decisions; takes responsibility.

Evidence of mastery. Consistent, fair decisions; equity gaps closing; high trust across the community.

Development activities. Analyze an ethical dilemma; run an equity audit; clarify your leadership values.

Suggested resources. Leadership Simulations · equity framework · decision-making rubric.

Growth progression. Emerging follows norms → Transforming shapes an ethical culture.

💡 Innovation Leadership

Change & future-readiness · Domain 8 of 8

Definition. Fostering a culture of learning, experimentation, and improvement to meet a changing future.

Leadership behaviors. Encourages smart risks; pilots and scales ideas; leads change; embraces new tools responsibly.

Evidence of mastery. Successful pilots adopted; staff propose ideas; the school adapts ahead of need.

Development activities. Run a small pilot; lead a change initiative; build an improvement-cycle routine.

Suggested resources. Educational Innovation · Leading Change · AI for School Leaders.

Growth progression. Emerging tries new ideas → Transforming builds a self-renewing organization.

Research note

Leadership is the second-greatest school-level influence on student learning, after teaching itself, and its effect is largest in the schools that need it most. Competency frameworks make that influence learnable: by naming observable behaviors and evidence of mastery, they let leaders self-assess, target development, and track growth over time. Each domain here is defined behaviorally and paired with a growth progression so it can be developed deliberately rather than left to experience alone. Framework is illustrative.

Growth Progression

Every competency is developed along the same five-stage arc.

Emerging

Aware of the competency; applies it with support and prompting.

Developing

Applies it consistently in familiar situations with growing independence.

Proficient

Applies it reliably and adapts to new and complex situations.

Leading

Models the competency and develops it in others.

Transforming

Embeds the competency into systems and culture at scale.
