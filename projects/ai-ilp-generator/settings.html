🛡️ Our commitment

Responsible AI & Ethics

This platform is built on the principle that AI supports educators — it never replaces professional judgment. Every recommendation is a draft for review.

👤 Human oversight

A qualified educator reviews, approves, and adapts every plan, goal, and report. AI proposes; people decide.

🔍 Transparency

We clearly label AI-generated drafts and disclose that outputs are simulated, illustrative, and based on fictional data.

💡 Explainability

Recommendations include a plain-language rationale (“why this recommendation”) so educators can evaluate the reasoning.

⚖️ Bias awareness

We actively watch for and mitigate bias so suggestions do not disadvantage any learner or group.

🔒 Privacy & student-data protection

Data minimization by design. In this demo, all data is fictional and stored only in your local browser — never transmitted.

🎚️ Responsible personalization

Personalization broadens opportunity; it never tracks learners into limiting paths or lowers expectations.

🌍 Equity

Tools are designed to widen access and close gaps, with attention to accessibility and diverse learner needs.

📝 Consent considerations

Real deployments require informed consent and clear communication with families about how AI is used.

✅ Appropriate educator review

No plan is shared or acted upon without an educator's explicit review and approval.

🚀 For leaders

Implementation guide

A concise framework for schools, districts, ministries of education, student-support and special-education teams, and curriculum leaders bringing responsible AI planning to their context.

  1. Implementation strategy. Start with a clear purpose, success criteria, and named owners. Position AI as an assistant that augments — never replaces — educator judgment.
  2. Teacher training. Build educator capacity in prompting, interpreting drafts, reviewing for bias, and the approval workflow before any classroom use.
  3. Responsible AI. Adopt the principles above as policy: human oversight, transparency, explainability, and equity, with a clear accountability chain.
  4. Privacy. Apply data minimization, role-based access, and family communication; align with FERPA, GDPR, and local data-protection law.
  5. Data governance. Define data ownership, retention, audit trails, and vendor agreements; review systems regularly for accuracy and fairness.
  6. Pilot. Run a small, time-boxed pilot with a representative cohort, baseline metrics, and structured educator and family feedback.
  7. Scaling roadmap. Expand in phases tied to readiness gates — training complete, governance signed off, and pilot outcomes met.
  8. Evaluation metrics. Track learning outcomes, goal-attainment rates, educator time saved, equity indicators, and stakeholder trust — and adjust.

Data

This demo stores everything in your browser's localStorage — nothing is sent to a server.

Local keys used: ilp:theme (appearance), ilp:library (saved drafts), ilp:hist (assistant history), and ilp:stats (session counts). Clearing data removes the library, history, and stats; your theme preference is unaffected.
