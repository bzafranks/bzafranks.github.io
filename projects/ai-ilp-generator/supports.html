Accommodations & Supports

The AI suggests instructional supports tailored to a learner's barriers — extended time, graphic organizers, visual supports, chunked assignments, alternative assessments, audio support, vocabulary scaffolds, flexible grouping, technology supports, and teacher prompts. Suggestions are a draft for educator review and must be aligned with each student's IEP/504 plan and school policy. All data is fictional.

Supports by category

Supports are organized into the four standard accommodation categories so they are easy to map onto an IEP/504 plan.

👁️ Presentation

How information reaches the learner.

  • Graphic organizers and concept maps
  • Visual supports, anchor charts, and icons
  • Audio support / text-to-speech for passages
  • Vocabulary scaffolds and pre-taught key terms
  • Chunked directions with teacher prompts

✏️ Response

How the learner shows what they know.

  • Sentence frames and word banks
  • Speech-to-text or scribe for extended writing
  • Alternative assessments (project, oral, portfolio)
  • Chunked assignments with check-ins
  • Use of a calculator or reference sheet where appropriate

🪑 Setting

Where learning and assessment happen.

  • Flexible, quiet workspace option
  • Preferential seating near support
  • Flexible grouping (small group / partner)
  • Reduced-distraction testing space
  • Technology supports (headphones, device access)

⏱️ Timing & Scheduling

When and for how long.

  • Extended time on extended-response tasks
  • Built-in movement or processing breaks
  • Tasks broken into shorter, timed segments
  • Most demanding work scheduled at peak focus times
  • Advance notice of transitions and deadlines

👤 Human-in-the-loop The AI is a planning assistant. Every support is a draft that educators review, approve, and align with IEP/504 plans and school policy. All data is fictional and illustrative.
