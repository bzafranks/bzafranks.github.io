Strategic Pillars

Nine Pillars of Transformation

Each pillar defines a vision, objectives, key initiatives, timeline, outcomes, success metrics, and stakeholders. Together they form a coherent national strategy.

🛡️ Pillar 1 · Responsible AI Governance

Vision: AI in Bahamian education is safe, ethical, transparent, and always under human oversight.

Objectives: establish national AI-in-education policy; ethics & bias safeguards; data governance; responsible procurement.

Key initiatives: national AI policy; an AI governance committee; vendor & data standards; academic-integrity guidance.

Timeline: Phase 1. Outcomes: trusted, compliant AI use. Metrics: policy adopted; % vendors compliant; incidents resolved. Stakeholders: Ministry, legal, schools, families. → Governance framework

📚 Pillar 2 · Teaching & Learning

Vision: AI amplifies great teaching and personalizes learning for every student.

Objectives: teacher AI assistants; personalized practice; tutoring support — always teacher-led.

Key initiatives: approved AI tools; lesson-planning & differentiation supports; pilots. Timeline: Phases 2–3. Outcomes: improved engagement & mastery. Metrics: learning gains; teacher adoption. Stakeholders: teachers, students.

🧩 Pillar 3 · Curriculum Modernization

Vision: the curriculum stays current, relevant, and AI-enhanced — aligned to national goals.

Objectives: AI-assisted curriculum design, resource generation, and career-connected learning.

Key initiatives: curriculum review cycles; AI literacy across subjects; technical pathways. Timeline: Phases 2–4. Outcomes: modern, coherent curriculum. Metrics: coverage; relevance ratings. Stakeholders: curriculum officers, teachers. → Curriculum transformation

📝 Pillar 4 · Assessment Transformation

Vision: assessment is authentic, timely, and informative — with human oversight on high stakes.

Objectives: automated feedback; adaptive & portfolio assessment; mastery tracking.

Key initiatives: AI-supported feedback tools; rubric libraries; integrity safeguards. Timeline: Phases 2–3. Outcomes: faster, richer feedback. Metrics: feedback turnaround; growth. Stakeholders: teachers, exam bodies. → Assessment innovation

🍎 Pillar 5 · Teacher Professional Learning

Vision: every Bahamian educator is confident and capable with AI.

Objectives: national AI PD framework; certification; coaching; communities of practice.

Key initiatives: tiered AI literacy courses; micro-credentials; champion network. Timeline: Phases 1–3. Outcomes: a capable workforce. Metrics: % certified; adoption. Stakeholders: teachers, colleges, unions. → Teacher development

🧭 Pillar 6 · Educational Leadership & Analytics

Vision: leaders make evidence-based decisions from classroom to ministry.

Objectives: role-based analytics; early-warning systems; school improvement support.

Key initiatives: the national educational-intelligence platform; data routines. Timeline: Phases 2–4. Outcomes: better decisions & equity. Metrics: usage; outcome trends. Stakeholders: leaders, ministry. → See Project #6

🎓 Pillar 7 · Student Success & AI Literacy

Vision: every student is a responsible, capable, future-ready user of AI.

Objectives: a K–12 AI literacy progression; digital citizenship; career preparation.

Key initiatives: AI literacy standards; creative & ethical use; safeguards for minors. Timeline: Phases 2–4. Outcomes: AI-fluent graduates. Metrics: AI literacy assessments. Stakeholders: students, families. → Student AI literacy

🏗️ Pillar 8 · Digital Infrastructure & Equity

Vision: AI-ready, secure infrastructure reaches every school — including the Family Islands.

Objectives: connectivity, devices, national platforms, offline access, security.

Key initiatives: national LMS & data platform; identity/SSO; Family-Island connectivity. Timeline: Phases 1–4. Outcomes: equitable access. Metrics: connectivity %, device ratios. Stakeholders: telecoms, ministry. → Infrastructure · Equity

🔬 Pillar 9 · Research & Innovation

Vision: The Bahamas learns from evidence and leads regional AI-in-education innovation.

Objectives: research partnerships; pilots with evaluation; knowledge sharing.

Key initiatives: university & international collaborations; an innovation fund; CARICOM exchange. Timeline: Phases 3–4. Outcomes: a learning system. Metrics: studies; pilots scaled. Stakeholders: universities, agencies. → Partnerships
