📋 Why certification matters

Certification turns the national leadership standards into a guarantee: every credentialed leader has demonstrated the competencies the system expects. Tied to renewal and continuing requirements, it keeps leadership current rather than a one-time milestone — and gives families, staff, and government a clear assurance of quality. 🔁 Renewal & continuing requirements — click to expand

▸Continuing professional developmentRequired

Credential holders complete continuing PD hours each renewal cycle — drawn from academy programs, microcredentials, and learning communities — to keep their practice current.

▸Professional portfolioEvidence

At renewal, leaders submit a professional portfolio of leadership impact — evidence mapped to the standards, reflection, and outcomes — reviewed by a certification panel.

▸Standing & lapsesStatus

Credentials in good standing remain active; those approaching expiry are flagged for renewal, and lapsed credentials follow a defined reinstatement pathway.

Professional standards

National professional standards and competency-based licensure are widely used to define and assure leadership quality. When certification is tied to demonstrated competencies and periodic renewal — rather than seat time alone — it supports continuous improvement and public confidence in school leadership.
