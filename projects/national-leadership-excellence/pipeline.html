🎯 Talent identification & readiness criteria — click to expand

▸How talent is identifiedProcess

A blend of nomination, performance evidence, and self-referral feeds a structured talent review. Identified educators complete a readiness assessment and enter a development pathway with a named sponsor.

  • Principal & peer nomination of high-potential teachers
  • Performance & growth evidence over multiple years
  • Open self-nomination to widen and diversify the pool
▸Leadership readiness assessmentCriteria

Readiness is rated against the national leadership standards across instructional, people, operational, and system competencies, producing a banding of Ready now, Ready 1–2 years, or Developing.

  • Instructional & school-improvement leadership
  • People leadership: coaching, teams, culture
  • Operational & resource stewardship
  • Ethics, equity, and system thinking
▸Career pathways & successionPathway

Each stage links to the next through defined pathways — preparation, certification, and academy programs — so movement is transparent. Succession plans map ready candidates to forecast vacancies, region by region.

Why a pipeline

In the leadership-and-retention research base, schools with strong principal supply experience fewer leadership gaps, faster recovery from turnover, and steadier improvement. A deliberate pipeline — rather than ad-hoc hiring — is the most reliable way to guarantee qualified leaders for every school. Illustrative summary; verify against current national data.
