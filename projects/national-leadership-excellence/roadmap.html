▸Phase 1 — National AssessmentComplete

Timeline: Year 1 · Governance: National Leadership Board commissions the diagnostic; Quality Assurance Office validates findings.

Milestones: system-wide capacity audit, regional gap analysis, vacancy & succession baseline, stakeholder consultation.

Performance indicators: baseline National Readiness Index established (62); five regions profiled; vacancy register completed.

▸Phase 2 — Leadership StandardsComplete

Timeline: Year 2 · Governance: Board approves standards; Regional Implementation Units consulted; QA Office benchmarks externally.

Milestones: draft & consult on competency framework, international benchmarking, ministerial adoption, regional briefings.

Performance indicators: national leadership standards published; 100% of regions briefed; standards mapped to certification.

▸Phase 3 — Leadership AcademyIn progress

Timeline: Year 3 · Governance: Academy accountable to the Board for certification quality; regional units nominate cohorts.

Milestones: academy established, Cohorts 1–3 delivered, certification pathway live, executive-development track launched.

Performance indicators: 1,240 leaders in the pipeline; certification rate at 78% (target 90%); 142 certified in Cohort 3.

▸Phase 4 — National RolloutPlanned

Timeline: Year 4 · Governance: Regional Implementation Units lead delivery; Board monitors equity across regions.

Milestones: all five regions onboarded, regional talent pools and succession plans active, mentoring scaled, under-served regions prioritised.

Performance indicators: 5/5 regions on track; succession readiness ≥ 70%; critical vacancies reduced a further 15%.

▸Phase 5 — Monitoring & Continuous ImprovementPlanned

Timeline: Year 5 · Governance: QA Office runs the evaluation cycle; Board publishes the annual national leadership report.

Milestones: embedded monitoring framework, biennial policy review, independent evaluation, continuous-improvement loop into the next cycle.

Performance indicators: National Readiness Index ≥ 85 (Leading); evidence-based review completed; sustained funding secured.

🏛️ Executive Brief — Ministers, Permanent Secretaries & Development Partners

Implementation strategy. This roadmap delivers a national leadership system in five sequenced phases over five years, moving deliberately from diagnosis (Phase 1) to standards (Phase 2), to capacity-building through a national academy (Phase 3), to national rollout across all regions (Phase 4), and finally to monitoring and continuous improvement (Phase 5).

Governance. A National Leadership Board owns policy, standards and funding; Regional Implementation Units deliver locally; an independent Quality Assurance Office safeguards integrity and reports risk directly to the Minister. Authority and accountability are explicit at every tier.

Capacity-building. The Leadership Academy is the engine of the strategy — preparing, certifying and continuously developing leaders against national standards, with regional talent pools and succession plans ensuring no critical post is left without a ready successor.

Funding. Delivery is underpinned by a secured multi-year funding envelope (≈ $240M over five years), allocated to the academy, pipeline, certification, regional implementation and governance — and tied to clear performance indicators rather than activity alone.

Monitoring & evaluation metrics. Progress is tracked against the National Readiness Index, certification rate, succession readiness, critical-vacancy reduction and regional equity. Phase 5 embeds independent evaluation and a biennial policy review so the system improves on each cycle.

5-year transformation. The expected outcome is a shift from a Foundational baseline (Index 62) to a Leading national leadership system (Index ≥ 85): higher certification, stronger succession continuity, fewer critical vacancies, and improved teacher retention and school performance — leadership treated as the central lever for national education outcomes.
