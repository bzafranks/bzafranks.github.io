🛤️ The academy learning journey

  • Stage 1 · Identify
    Selection & onboarding — identified leaders enter a cohort with a readiness baseline and learning plan.
  • Stage 2 · Build
    Core preparation — coursework and professional learning aligned to the national leadership standards.
  • Stage 3 · Apply
    Simulations & practicum — scenario practice and a supervised on-the-job leadership project.
  • Stage 4 · Coach
    Coaching & mentoring — individualized support from an executive coach and a peer mentor.
  • Stage 5 · Certify
    Certification & placement — competency assessment, credential, and placement into the pipeline.
  • Ongoing
    Continuous PD — renewal-linked learning that sustains and grows leadership over a career.

🎮 Inside a leadership simulation — click to expand

▸The scenarioPracticum

A new principal arrives to a sudden teacher shortage, a strained budget, and an anxious community. Over a simulated term, participants make staffing, communication, and resourcing decisions and see the consequences ripple through the school.

▸What it developsCompetencies

Decision-making under pressure, ethical reasoning, stakeholder communication, and the ability to keep instruction central in a crisis — practiced safely before the stakes are real.

  • Crisis & change leadership
  • People & community leadership
  • Resource stewardship & trade-offs
▸How it is assessedFeedback

Coaches review each decision branch with the participant against the national leadership standards, producing a personalized growth report that feeds the next stage of the journey.

Adult learning & leadership preparation

Effective leadership preparation reflects what is known about adult learning: it is experiential, problem-centered, and job-embedded, pairing rigorous coursework with coaching, practice, and reflection. Programs that blend simulations, mentoring, and authentic projects show stronger transfer to practice than coursework alone. Illustrative summary; verify against current research.
