Governance & Accountability

A strategy is only as strong as the governance that holds it. This system defines strategic review meetings, board reporting, leadership accountability, evidence tracking, decision logs, policy alignment, and a governance calendar — turning oversight into a continuous-improvement engine rather than a compliance exercise.

Research Foundation

Grounded in corporate & educational governance research, the Balanced Scorecard's strategic-review discipline, and evidence-based decision-making — clear roles, regular evidence review, transparent decision records, and aligned policy sustain accountability and trust. Plans, logs, and data are illustrative.

Governance Calendar

A predictable cadence of monthly, quarterly, and annual touchpoints keeps oversight rhythmic and proactive.

  • Monthly · Leadership review
    Leadership team reviews KPI progress, risks, and decisions; updates the decision log and evidence tracker.
  • Quarterly · Board report & strategic review
    Board receives the KPI dashboard, risk register, and decision summary; formal strategic-progress review.
  • Quarterly · Policy alignment check
    Confirm policies and resource decisions remain aligned to the strategic priorities.
  • Annual · Continuous-improvement review
    Full evaluation of outcomes against the plan; capture lessons learned and renew the strategy.

Accountability Roles

Clear decision rights prevent diffusion of responsibility. Expand each role to see its remit.

▸Board of Directors / Trustees

Sets strategic direction, approves major decisions and budgets, and holds leadership accountable for outcomes.

Receives the quarterly dashboard, risk register, and decision summary; conducts the annual strategy review.

▸Executive / Head of Organization

Owns delivery of the strategic plan; chairs the steering group; reports to the board.

Ensures evidence is tracked and decisions are logged and communicated.

▸Steering Group

Oversees initiative delivery, allocates resources, and unblocks issues between board meetings.

Maintains the risk register and recommends decisions for board approval.

▸Initiative Owners

Accountable for specific KPIs, milestones, evidence, and risks within their initiative.

Report status into the monthly leadership review. See the roadmap →

Why Governance Matters

Good governance is the difference between a plan that lives and one that gathers dust. Regular evidence review, transparent decision logs, named accountability, and aligned policy convert oversight into continuous improvement — building stakeholder trust and protecting the strategy from drift.
