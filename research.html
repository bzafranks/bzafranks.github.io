Doctoral Research

The Influence of Leadership Behaviors on Teacher Retention

🎓

The anchor of the Leadership Suite

The Influence of Leadership Behaviors on Teacher Retention: A Basic Qualitative Study — my doctoral dissertation (EdD in Leadership, American College of Education, 2025) — investigates how the behaviors of school leaders shape whether teachers choose to stay, examining the connection between leadership practice, school culture, and the retention of effective educators. The work asks a question with high stakes for every school: what do leaders actually do that keeps great teachers in the building?

This research runs as a through-line across the entire Educational Leadership & School Improvement Suite. It directly anchors the Teacher Retention & Workforce Excellence System and informs the design of the leadership development, coaching, and culture frameworks throughout the suite — translating findings about leadership behavior into practical, adoptable systems.

Applied Research Series

Studies on international students & workforce outcomes

As Chief Research Officer at Virtuous Sea Corporation, I lead applied research connecting international education, employer expectations, and labor-market outcomes — directing mixed-methods studies and a multi-year research agenda, and translating findings into actionable insight for institutions, employers, and policymakers.

🌏

International Student Labor-Market Studies

Applied research examining how Chinese international students transition into the labor market — the alignment between their education, expectations, and employment outcomes, and the implications for how programs prepare students for work.

🤝

Employer Perception Studies

Research into how employers perceive graduates' readiness, skills, and credentials — surfacing the gaps between what education systems produce and what the workforce actually values.

Applied Research Series

A continuing series of applied studies bridging education and the world of work — bringing an evidence base to decisions about career pathways, skills, and program design.
