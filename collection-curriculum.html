Why this collection matters

Great standards still need great learning design

Most schools have standards. What they lack are complete, classroom-ready learning systems that any teacher — or any learner working independently — can pick up and run with fidelity.

This collection demonstrates curriculum built as a system: clear learning targets, scaffolded instruction, embedded assessment, accessibility from the first draft, and delivery models that hold up during teacher shortages and remote disruption. Each project shows how design choices — asynchronous self-pacing, hybrid continuity, competency-based progression, AI personalization, and UDL — solve a specific instructional problem while keeping rigor and equity at the center.

Designed for continuity

Every curriculum here is usable in school or virtually, with or without a teacher present — so learning continues even when staffing does not.
